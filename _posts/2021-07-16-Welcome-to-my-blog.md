Welcome to my blog. Since this website is pretty new and I'm currently experimenting with GitHub pages, some stuff might get changed later on. I plan to use the website for a blog of mine regarding
InfoSec research, programming projects or other subjects I participate in. For all the people who don't know anything about me yet (I guess that applies for most of the readers) - I usually like reading
about InfoSec topics or programming theory in my free time. I currently don't spend much time coding projects on my own, rather I enjoy reading write-ups by the Community about their projects. I plan to change
that, hence I created this blog. In case I work on projects myself, I mostly use Python - especially regarding the automation of smaller tasks - though I might release future projects in Golang or other 'exotic'
programming languages, as I like to call them, especially since Golang is highly appreciated in the InfoSec Community for its' amazing concurrency system and lightning fast speeds. I'm currently employed as a
backend developer and attend an university of applied sciences, with a Bachelor of Engineering in applied informatics (actual course of studies name too complex) as goal. In my free time I also enjoy professional
photography and as mentioned before I appreciate all contributions from community members. Combining those two factors almost force me to mention a certain member of the community to whom I highly look up to: STOK.
As a bug bounty hunter he always releases interesting videos regarding that field, and the video quality proves to be astounding every single time I watch a video. Having STOK as an idol, my plans include contributing
to the awesome Community as well and getting to know as many people as possible. I am looking forward for collaborations and teamwork - happy to be a part of this. Thanks for reading my first blog post :)
